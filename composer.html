Biography

Kai-Young Chan is an Associate Professor of music composition and an academic scholar whose work sits at the vital intersection of linguistic nuance, cultural heritage, and contemporary art music.

His creative practice focus centers on turning linguistic rules into innovative creative assets, transforming structural text-setting boundaries into expressive melodic tools. He is a recipient of the prestigious CASH Golden Sail Music Award, and his compositions are regularly performed globally by ensembles including the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra and the Hong Kong Philharmonic.

His scores are published internationally by Universal Edition (Vienna), Edition Peters (London), and Edition ICOT (Japan).

Research Projects

From Constraints to Creativity

Serving as Principal Investigator, this ongoing project investigates how Hong Kong composers navigate linguistic speech-tone structures to yield highly clear, expressive vocal music frameworks.

This research infrastructure led to the development of the open-access interactive platform Cantonese Melody Generator.
