Biography

Kai-Young Chan is an Associate Professor of music composition and an academic scholar whose work sits at the vital intersection of linguistic nuance, cultural heritage, and contemporary art music.

His creative practice focuses on turning linguistic rules into innovative creative assets, transforming structural text-setting boundaries into expressive melodic tools. He is a recipient of the CASH Golden Sail Music Award, and his compositions are performed globally by the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra and the Hong Kong Philharmonic.
